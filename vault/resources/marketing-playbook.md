# Brandon Willington — Complete Playbook (Meta Ads + Landing Pages)
*Merged July 2026 from two sources: the Meta Ads Lead Gen Guide and the Where U Landing Page Research. This file supersedes both. For the condensed cross-marketer summary, see `marketing/market-intelligence.md` §2/§7 — that file now points here for depth.*

**Core philosophy: Efficiency Over Spend.** Lead gen from Meta is a systems problem, not a spend problem. If you pay $1/click and the page converts at 10%, CPL is $10. Get the page to 20% and CPL is $5 — lead flow doubled without an extra dollar of ad spend. When leads dry up, the problem is almost always the landing page or the mismatch between ad hook and page message, not the ads.

**How this file is organised:** Section A is the ad side (getting the right click). Sections 1–10 are the landing page / funnel side (converting the click). Willington's diagnostic rule ties them together: **chase the problem upstream** — low close rate → check conversions first; low conversions → check clicks first. Most businesses notice the problem at "close" and try to fix it there when the real break is two steps earlier.

| Pillar | Job | Symptom when broken |
|---|---|---|
| Clicks | Get the right person to click the ad | High CTR but low-quality / out-of-area leads |
| Conversions | Turn the visitor into a lead on the page | Clicks but no leads, high bounce |
| Close | Turn the lead into a paying client | Ghosting, no-shows, low close rate |

## PART A — META ADS & CREATIVE

### A1. The Curious vs Committed spectrum
All traffic sits on a 0–10 spectrum. A suspiciously high CTR (above ~6%) usually means you're attracting *curious* people who clicked out of interest and will never fill a form — not *committed* people who expect a sales pitch and are ready to hand over contact details. The goal is never maximum clicks. It is maximum **qualified** clicks.

### A2. The 2.5-line rule
95% of people read only the first 2.5 lines of primary text before "See more…". The hook must qualify (or disqualify) the right person in those lines. It is the most valuable real estate in the entire ad.

### A3. The hook framework — Attention + Reason to Continue
A hook is not "watch me." It is "watch me… because I'm about to show you something useful."
1. **Call out the person.** Make it obvious who this is for — filters the right people in, wrong people out. *"If you're a business owner spending money on Facebook ads…"*
2. **Introduce a tension or problem.** Something might be wrong, missing, or misunderstood. *"…there's a good chance you're testing ads the wrong way."*
3. **Promise the payoff.** The step most people forget. *"…and I'll show you the exact hook structure we use to generate enquiries."*

### A4. The Implied Service rule
The hook must imply what the service is. Close the loop immediately — big open loops attract the curious.
- Weak (open loop): "This is how businesses are growing faster than ever before."
- Strong (closed loop): "This is the Meta ads strategy we use to grow service businesses."
Words like *client*, *my services*, *leads* set the expectation a transaction is on the table — filters out the curious, leaves the committed.

### A5. The 5-second video rule
The first 5 seconds must set clear expectations. No unrelated viral memes or emotional bait for cheap clicks — the goal is not to go viral, it's to attract the right person. "Most of your life is just managing expectations": set the bar low and clear publicly so click-through expectations are met or exceeded.

### A6. Filming ads that convert
- **Don't script.** Reading a script kills authenticity — speak conversationally.
- **Use your face.** People buy from people; faces build trust fastest.
- **Vary formats.** Run talking head, testimonial, B-roll + voiceover, before/after, and text-image ads simultaneously. Never rely on a single premise.

### A7. Managing lead quality vs quantity (resistance)
Lead quality is controlled by manipulating **resistance**:
- *Physical resistance* — clicks, keystrokes, scrolls required to become a lead.
- *Psychological resistance* — thought required to answer questions; trust required to proceed.

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Too many low-quality leads | Too little resistance | Add long-form fields, more qualifying questions |
| Too few leads overall | Too much resistance | Remove unnecessary fields, simplify |
| Out-of-area leads | Hook/targeting doesn't imply service area | Add location language to hooks and headline |

The **Fashion vs Function** principle: a page that is all fashion (pretty) with no function (clear conversion path) gets no leads; all function with no fashion looks like a spammy pop-up and destroys trust. Sit in the middle.

### A8. The 3 most common Meta ads mistakes
1. **Wrong objective.** Optimising for Link Clicks / Landing Page Views instead of Leads or Schedules. Meta does what you tell it — optimise for clicks and it finds clickers, not buyers.
2. **Running one ad.** The market has multiple segments with different pains. Test multiple angles, hooks and formats simultaneously.
3. **Sending traffic to the homepage.** Ads need a dedicated landing page with all distractions removed and one CTA.

### A9. Post-conversion nurture (why leads ghost)
Excitement peaks at booking and drops hard over the next 72 hours.
- Don't let leads book calls more than **3–5 days out**.
- Send communication between booking and call to push excitement back up.
- **Industry-matched case studies** — an electrician gets an electrician case study, not a PT one.
- **Long-form content for parasocial bonding** — podcast appearances, long YouTube videos. The lead spends time with you virtually and arrives at the call already trusting you.

### A10. Ad hook audit checklist
- [ ] Implied service — is it clear what's being offered?
- [ ] Calls out the person — obvious who it's for?
- [ ] Introduces tension — a problem, gap, or risk named?
- [ ] Promises the payoff — exactly what they'll learn or gain?
- [ ] 2.5-line test — right person qualified in the first 2.5 lines?
- [ ] 5-second test — video sets expectations immediately?
- [ ] Multiple angles running, not one creative?
- [ ] Campaign optimised for Leads/Schedules, not clicks?

### A11. DIY speed-build workflow (from the 2026 "build it in a weekend" video)
*Source: Willington YouTube, transcribed July 2026. Context: 1,500+ AU service businesses through the 2-day With U workshops; members regularly post leads the evening they leave.*

**Step 1 — Generate 15+ ad ideas, split by motivator direction.**
Post the **Andromeda algorithm update**, launch with **at least a dozen ads** (he targets 15) so the algorithm has options to choose from — the old 1–3 ad launch is dead. Generate ideas with AI using a **towards / away-from motivator split**:
- *Towards* — goals and aspirations the person moves toward (e.g. "the future you", "confidence upgrade", "train like an athlete", "your comeback story").
- *Away-from* — things they're escaping or want to avoid through inaction (e.g. "the slow decline", "the I'll-start-Monday chat", "the stairs test", "don't wait for the doctor's warning").

**Step 2 — AI-draft the primary text, forcing a committed first sentence.**
Unprompted AI writes curious hooks. Constrain it explicitly: *"Make the first sentence more committed than curious — a committed sentence implies this is for [service]; a curious one leaves the solution open."*
- Curious: "This is how guys are losing stubborn belly fat."
- Committed: "This is how **our members** are losing stubborn belly fat."
AI output gets you 80–90% there; tinker in the business-specific details (24/7 access, small group classes, etc.) yourself — never run it copy-paste.

**Step 3 — Content: film fast, or don't film at all.**
- Camera-shy option: client photos + Canva text overlay of the ad's main idea. Fully valid.
- Filming: phone is fine. **Movement triggers flow state** — walk around while talking, fidget a pen/marker; a static rigid body produces a static mind and brain-farts. Brick-and-mortar: film out the front so people recognise the location.
- Formula per ad: say the first sentence (the idea), talk about your business for ~30 seconds, end with "click below to learn more."
- Don't overthink delivery — he filmed 17 ads in 15 minutes; one had a botched CTA ("I'll see you on Zoom… I'll see you Thursday") and still converted. Cut the ums in post or don't.

**Step 4 — Landing page from pre-built sections (~1 hour).**
HighLevel drag-and-drop pre-built sections, in this order: **welcome section (headline + video + form) → testimonials → FAQs → our-team/who-am-I**. These are the same sections on his own live pages. Fill in headline, drop in a business video, populate testimonials — done. (Fashion vs function warning applies — see A7; over-designed pages with loading animations add resistance and kill function.)

**Step 5 — Sales: speed wins, time kills.**
**Call every lead within 5 minutes** — that's the peak of their curiosity and commitment. The proof case: an untrained 22-year-old rep given only the 45% of applicants who *started but never booked a call*, with an automation that auto-dialled him 5–10 minutes after an abandoned application ("Hey Jake, connecting you to Aiden, he's in the content industry, good luck"). Zero training, pure speed → **matched the trained team's 50% close rate**; once on the booking calendar, closed **70–75%**. A Harvard study backs it: close likelihood decays continuously from the moment of enquiry.
- Actionable automation: application started + no call booked within 5–10 min → auto-dial the owner/rep with lead context.

### A12. Image ads & voice-of-customer mining (from the 2026 "cheap and dirty image ads" video)
*Source: Willington YouTube, transcribed July 2026. The camera-free alternative — image ads still convert effectively.*

**The mistake:** random photo + offer dump ("Join this gym, $24.70/week, feature, feature, feature").

**The method: repeat the prospect's internal monologue back to them.**
Don't call out demographics ("Are you an 18–24 year old male who wants to learn to journal?") — that *feels like being advertised to*. Instead, put the exact words already running through their head on the image. The reaction you're engineering: *"how are you reading my mind right now?"* Feeling understood builds affinity; being targeted builds resistance.

**Origin story (validation workflow worth copying):** before building anything for a journaling app, he posted an Instagram story — *"If you've been trying to build the habit of journaling but struggle to do it consistently, message me and tell me why."* The DM replies ("I stare at a blank page and don't know what to write") became the image ads, verbatim. Cheap landing page template + square images of customer language = validated demand before a line of code.

**Anatomy of a working image ad** (his live webinar examples):
- **Qualifier + motivator in one line:** "For service-based business owners who want to get more leads than their agency ever got them" — *service-based* filters out e-comm; *more than their agency ever got them* fires the away-from motivator (escaping done-for-you agencies).
- Other live examples: "…who want to stop relying on word of mouth for leads" · "…who want more qualified leads, not just tyre-kickers and no-shows." Each names a specific pain the reader self-identifies with: *"ah, that's me."*
- **Logistics line:** "This Wednesday, 12pm AEDT, live on Zoom" — clear, concrete, low-friction.
- **Humans in the image.** Same text on a plain/gradient background vs. with humans in it — the human version wins *significantly*. Identical principle to the landing-page background hierarchy (see Element 2): humans build trust.

**Where to mine the monologue (in priority order):**
1. What prospects say on **sales calls** — especially objections.
2. **Comments** on your content.
3. **Testimonials** — the language clients use to describe the before-state.
4. What clients reveal **weeks or months into the program** — the real reasons ("outstanding tax bills", "missed payroll once") surface after the sale, never during it.

**Scaling rule:** *if one person said it, a thousand people thought it.* Don't dismiss a verbatim line as a niche one-off — his ATO-debt confession pulled a flood of "I'm in the same situation, if not worse" responses. The personal, slightly uncomfortable triggers are where the gold is.

### A13. Landing-page quick wins (from the 2026 "grow your service business" video)
*Source: Willington YouTube, transcribed July 2026. Conversion-side companion to Part A's ad-side material; overlaps Elements 1–6 below but adds specific layouts, numbers, and one live split-test result. Fastest With U member win cited: 24 hours, 3 leads/week → 3 leads/day, from landing-page changes alone.*

**Why the landing page is the easiest lever.** Traffic constant + conversion 2.5% → 5% = the business doubles overnight with zero extra ad spend. (Same efficiency-over-spend logic as the core philosophy — the page is upstream of everything except the click.)

**The split-hero layout.** Kill the "headline = business name, subheadline = business name restated" pattern ("Perth Electrician / We are electricians in Perth"). Split the above-the-fold section in two: headline + subheadline on the left, **CTA form fields on the right**. On mobile this stacks as headline → sub → form with *zero scrolling to start*. Never a low-contrast "Contact" button that loads a second page — every click, keystroke and scroll is physical resistance (3 seconds to find a button is an eternity with TikTok brain).

**The survey widget (HighLevel).** Replace the flat name/email/mobile form with a multi-step survey so the visitor feels they're *starting a process*:
1. **Radio-select opener: "Which best describes you?"** — self-centred question, people are naturally interested in themselves, and radio beats checkbox for the exam-psychology reason: everyone does the multiple-choice section first because the answer is already on the page.
2. **Qualification slide** ("Which industry are you in?") — and here's the lead-quality fix: wrong-industry selections simply don't pass through. Hard-filter at the form, not on the sales call.
3. Optional third radio question, then name/email/mobile — all slides on one page, no reloads.

**His ballpark conversion math** (he estimates pages ±1%): business-name headline + buried contact form ≈ **1.5–2%**; split-hero + survey ≈ **5–7.5%** — i.e. 3–4× the leads from layout alone.

**Owner on the page, above the fold.** 30 seconds of video saying *almost anything* ("Hi, I'm Brandon, I own this agency, fill out the form and I'll be in touch") — reported 100% success rate improving conversion. Stock images generate unanswered questions ("is this real? who are these people? why won't they show their face?"); a real face answers them.

**The background-image split test (Industry Set, his music-education business).** A forgotten HighLevel split test where the only change was the barely-visible background image: stock producer-in-bedroom → photo of him and his co-founder presenting to a room of *a dozen people*. **Conversion rate doubled.** The subconscious read: they're real, and people listen to them in rooms. His first audit question is now "do you have a photo of you talking to a crowd?" — even a best-man speech photo works. (Extends the Element 2 background hierarchy with a hard data point.)

**Google reviews placement.** Usually placed too far down. Put them immediately after the hero/CTA, matching the visitor's thought sequence: *this is what you do → you're real → this is what you want me to do →* **"…but has anyone else done this?"** — reviews answer that exact thought and kill the guinea-pig objection at the moment it occurs.

**The internal-notification workflow (speed-to-lead, implemented).** In HighLevel: form completion → **"internal notification" workflow action → SMS to your phone** containing the lead's name, mobile, and every custom field they answered. You read the text, know everything, and tap their number to call — no laptop, no CRM login, no email-inbox distraction detour. This is the concrete build spec for the 5-minute rule in §A11 Step 5.

### A14. Application notes — Pinnacle / VitalEdge
- **Pinnacle (B2C client acquisition):** hooks should call out the situation, matching the soul file's audience table — *"If you've trained for years and still look the same…"* is already a perfect Willington Step-1 call-out. Location language ("Hamilton", "local") on any in-person/free-session offers to kill out-of-area leads.
- **VitalEdge (B2B coach acquisition):** implied service matters double — "coaching platform" must appear in the hook or you attract lifters, not coaches. Qualifying friction is a feature: coaches with clients are the committed end of the spectrum.
- **Both:** external checkout / application pages are the dedicated landing page — never send ad traffic to the homepage.
- **Speed-to-lead (both):** wire the GHL **internal-notification** workflow (§A13) — form completion/abandonment → SMS to Christian's phone with name, mobile, and all custom fields, tap-to-call. Triple-sourced now: Willington's Jake data, Hormozi's "triples sales," and this video's exact build spec. Highest-leverage item in the playbook; it's one workflow.
- **Ad volume:** any new Meta campaign launches with 12–15 creatives minimum (Andromeda-era requirement). Batch-film walking the Adonis Gym floor — location recognition + flow state in one move.
- **Voice-of-customer bank (both brands):** mine Pinnacle weekly check-ins, consult form answers, and DM threads for verbatim client language ("trained for years and still look the same", "I feel hip thrusts in my lower back") — these become the image-ad copy bank and feed the soul file's "words the ideal client uses". For VitalEdge, mine coach conversations: "Kahunas keeps breaking", "my clients lie on MyFitnessPal" — competitor-pain-points-2026.md is already a pre-built monologue source.
- **Image-ad format for Pinnacle:** real Adonis Gym client photos (consented) + one monologue line + qualifier. Camera-free content stream that matches the soul file's testimonial rules.

## PART 3 — BRANDON / WHERE U LANDING PAGE PRINCIPLES

### PRINCIPLE 1: Video Above the Fold — Earn Attention Before Asking for the Form
**Source:** https://ahoy.whereu.com.au/withu (confirmed from page)
**Quote/Finding:** The Where U page opens with "CLICK BELOW TO WATCH FIRST!" before any form is shown. *Inferred from page structure.*
**Why it matters:** Video pre-sells, answers objections, builds trust, and warms the lead before any data is requested. This dramatically reduces form abandonment by ensuring only interested, informed people submit.
**How to apply to fitness coaching:** Place a 60–90 second personal video at the top of your coaching page. Talk directly to the kind of client you want. Show your personality, your results, your process. Let the video do the qualification work. Only show the booking/application form AFTER the video.

---

### PRINCIPLE 2: The 3-Pillar Funnel Diagnostic — Ads → Page → Sales Conversation
**Source:** Grit Daily Podcast, Addicted2Success, Barid TV articles (confirmed)
**Quote:** *"Clicks from the right person. Landing pages that convert. Conversations that close."*
**Why it matters:** This tells you exactly where to fix problems. If your ad CPL is high, fix the ad. If your page conversion rate is low, fix the page. If your close rate is low, fix the sales call. Stop guessing.
**How to apply:** Track all three stages in your coaching funnel. Your landing page's job is NOT to close — it is ONLY to convert a qualified visitor into a booked call or completed application. Keep this singular focus.

---

### PRINCIPLE 3: Qualifying Form, Not Just Lead Capture
**Source:** https://ahoy.whereu.com.au/withu (confirmed from page structure)
**Finding:** Where U's form asks: business type, revenue range, lead source, learning style preference, website URL, name, email, phone. *Confirmed from direct page analysis.*
**Why it matters:** This pre-qualifies leads before they enter your sales conversation. You stop wasting time on leads who are wrong for your program. Higher quality leads = higher close rate = lower cost per acquisition.
**How to apply:** For your coaching page, consider a multi-step application form that asks:
- What is your main goal? (Build muscle / Lose fat / Improve performance / Rehab an injury)
- How long have you been training? (Beginner / 1–3 years / 3+ years)
- What's your biggest frustration right now?
- Are you ready to invest in professional coaching?
- Name / Email / Phone

### PRINCIPLE 4: Proof Volume — More Than Anyone Else In The Industry
**Source:** https://ahoy.whereu.com.au/withu (confirmed — page contains 100+ testimonial screenshots)
**Headline used:** *"More Proof Than Literally Anyone Else In The Industry..."*
**Quote from page:** *"The Results Your Last 'Results Driven Agency' Wasn't Able To Deliver"*
**Why it matters:** Social proof at scale removes doubt. The sheer volume of screenshots communicates: "This is not hand-picked. This is what happens regularly."
**How to apply:** For fitness coaching, collect screenshots of: client weight/measurement changes, body composition photos, client texts about results, blood work improvements, milestone achievements. Aim for 20–50+ visible testimonials. Do NOT curate to just your best 5. Volume is credibility.

### PRINCIPLE 5: FAQ as Objection Crusher (Not Just Information)
**Source:** https://ahoy.whereu.com.au/withu (confirmed — extensive FAQ section)
**Finding:** Where U's FAQ directly addresses: cost concerns, time commitment, tech skill level, whether it works for their industry, upsells, and what happens if results don't come. Every FAQ is a disguised objection. *Confirmed from page.*
**Why it matters:** People who are almost-ready-to-buy stay on the page because their specific hesitation is answered in a conversational, honest way. FAQ sections lower bounce rates and increase form completion.
**How to apply for fitness coaching:**
- "Is this for beginners or advanced lifters?"
- "What if I have an old injury?"
- "How much time do I need per week?"
- "Do you offer online coaching?"
- "How long until I see results?"
- "What makes this different from a regular PT?"
- "How much does it cost?"
- "What if I want to cancel?"

### PRINCIPLE 6: No Upsells — Build Trust Through Transparency
**Source:** https://ahoy.whereu.com.au/withu (confirmed)
**Quote:** *"One of Brandon's intro slides the morning of day 1 says 'fkn relax, there's no upsells here.'"*
**Why it matters:** Pre-emptively addressing the "what's the catch?" objection that every premium offer faces. Saying "there are no upsells" removes a major psychological barrier.
**How to apply:** On your coaching page, be direct about what's included and what isn't. If you have a premium tier, say so clearly upfront and frame it as a choice, not a pressure.

### PRINCIPLE 7: Conversational Sales Process — Not Pushy, Not Scripted
**Source:** Grit Daily podcast, ahoy.whereu.com.au/withu FAQ (confirmed)
**Quote:** *"Brandon teaches a super conversational way to close more deals... members reach back out and say they actually just closed more of the leads they already had before even running any ads."*
**Why it matters:** The discovery call / strategy call is an extension of the landing page experience. If the page was honest and clear, the call should feel natural, not like a hard sell.
**How to apply:** Your coaching call should start with curiosity about the client, not a pitch. Ask: What are they trying to achieve? What have they tried? What's stopped them? Then present your solution in the context of their specific situation.

### PRINCIPLE 8: One Offer, One Focus — Ruthless Simplicity
**Source:** Addicted2Success, Barid TV (confirmed)

### 1. HERO SECTION

**Goal:** Instantly communicate what transformation you deliver, who it's for, and compel them to keep reading.

**What to write:**
- Headline: Transformation promise for your specific ideal client
- Sub-headline: How you deliver it + who it's for
- Visual: Professional photo of you OR a client transformation

**Visuals:** Your training photo or a strong transformation side-by-side. Video preferred.

**Common mistakes to avoid:**
- Generic "Transform Your Body!" headlines — be specific
- No image of you — people hire the coach, not the logo
- Nav bar with 10 links — remove it entirely from a landing page

---

### 2. PAIN / PROBLEM SECTION

**Goal:** Name the exact frustrations your ideal client feels. Make them feel seen before you offer anything.

**What to write:**
List 4–6 specific pains your clients express before they work with you.

**Example copy:**
> *You've been training consistently — but your body still doesn't look like it should.*
>
> - You're not sure which exercises are actually building the physique you want
> - You've tried multiple programs and nothing seems to stick
> - You worry an old injury is holding you back, but no one has properly addressed it
> - Your nutrition feels random — some days you're dialled, most days you're not
> - You know what a good body looks like. You just can't seem to build one.

**CTA:** None here — don't interrupt the empathy flow.

**Common mistakes to avoid:** Vague problems ("lack of motivation") — use specific, client-verbatim language.

---

### 3. DESIRED OUTCOME SECTION

**Goal:** Paint a vivid picture of what your clients' lives/bodies look like after working with you.

**What to write:**
Mirror the pain points with outcomes. Concrete and visual.

**Example copy:**
> *Here's what changes when you have the right coach:*
>
> - You know exactly what to eat, how to train, and why — no more guessing
> - Your physique responds predictably to your effort
> - Your injury stops holding you back — rehab is built into your program
> - Your blood work matches your training, your nutrition works WITH your hormones
> - You walk into a competition — or the gym — with confidence in your body

**Visuals:** Transformation photos with client names/stories.

---

### 4. UNIQUE MECHANISM — WHY YOUR COACHING IS DIFFERENT

**Goal:** Explain the specific thing you do that others don't. Your competitive advantage, made tangible.

**What to write:**
Name your coaching method or approach. Be specific.

**Example copy:**
> *Most coaches give you a program and disappear. I don't.*
>
> *My coaching combines evidence-based hypertrophy programming with posing instruction, injury-aware training design, precision nutrition coaching, and blood work education — so your body builds the physique you're actually after, not just the one a generic plan allows.*
>
> *This isn't fitness advice. This is precision coaching.*

---

### 5. WHO IT'S FOR / WHO IT'S NOT FOR

**Goal:** Pre-qualify the visitor. Make the right people feel seen. Let the wrong people self-select out.

**What to write:**

> **This coaching is for you if:**
> - You're serious about building a premium physique and are ready to invest in doing it properly
> - You've been training for 1+ years and want expert guidance to take things further
> - You want a coach who understands hypertrophy, posing, nutrition AND injury rehab
> - You're looking for structure, accountability, and real feedback — not just a plan in your inbox
>
> **This coaching is NOT for you if:**
> - You want a quick fix or weight-loss-only solution
> - You're not ready to track, measure, and engage consistently
> - You expect results without doing the work

---

### 6. TRANSFORMATION / OFFER SECTION

**Goal:** Describe exactly what clients get. Make the value concrete and the investment feel obvious.

**What to write:**
Name everything included. Use specifics, not vague descriptions.

**Example copy:**
> *Here's what's included in your coaching program:*
>
> ✅ Fully customised hypertrophy training program (revised every 4–8 weeks)
> ✅ Detailed nutrition plan — calories, macros, meal timing, supplementation
> ✅ Posing and physique development coaching (competition-ready or aesthetics-focused)
> ✅ Injury rehab integration — your old shoulder/back/knee issue gets addressed, not ignored
> ✅ Blood work education and analysis (understand what your labs are actually saying)
> ✅ Weekly check-ins with direct coaching feedback
> ✅ 24/7 messaging access for questions, adjustments, and accountability

---

### 7. PROOF / TESTIMONIALS / RESULTS SECTION

**Goal:** Social proof at volume. Quantity + quality. Make the transformation feel normal, not rare.

**What to write:**
"Real results from real clients" — then show them.

**What to include:**
- Before/after physique photos (with client permission)
- Screenshot testimonials from messages
- Specific metric changes ("gained 8kg of muscle in 6 months")
- Video testimonials where possible
- Blood work transformation examples
- Competition results

**Heading option:** *"Results That Speak For Themselves"*
**Sub-heading:** *"[N] clients. [N] transformations. No cherry-picking — these are just the ones who said yes to sharing."*

---

### 8. COACHING PROCESS SECTION

**Goal:** Show how easy and clear the process is. Reduce perceived friction/complexity.

**What to write:**
3–5 step process, simple and sequential.

**Example:**
> **Step 1:** Apply online (takes 3 minutes)
> **Step 2:** We have a 20-minute discovery call to make sure we're the right fit
> **Step 3:** Your custom program is built and delivered within 48 hours
> **Step 4:** We check in weekly, adjust constantly, and you start building the physique you've been working toward

---

### 9. OBJECTION-HANDLING SECTION

**Goal:** Address the hesitations that are stopping your ideal client from clicking Apply right now.

Build as a FAQ section. See Part 5 for specific fitness-oriented FAQ questions.

---

### 10. PRICING / VALUE FRAMING SECTION

**Goal:** If you include pricing, frame value first. Never lead with price.

**What to write:**
- What's the cost of NOT investing? (wasted years, plateau, injury risk)
- What's the investment?
- Framing: "Less than one PT session per day" or "Cheaper than a gym membership you're underusing"
- Guarantee or risk reversal if applicable

**Common mistakes to avoid:** Listing prices before value. Hiding prices entirely (creates trust issues for high-ticket).

**Goal:** Convert. Clear, compelling, low-friction.

**What to write:**
Restate the transformation. Then give them one clear action.

**Example copy:**

> *Applications take 3 minutes. Spots are limited.*

> **[Apply for Coaching →]**

| **Landing Page CVR** | % of visitors who complete the form/book a call | Core health of the page | <2% = serious page issue | Fix headline, proof, CTA, or form |
| **Cost Per Lead (CPL)** | Ad spend ÷ number of leads | Efficiency of ad + page combined | CPL rising = ad or page degrading | Test new ad creative or page headline |
| **Cost Per Booked Call** | Ad spend ÷ number of booked calls | Value of each call funnel | High = low show-up rate or low form-to-call CVR | Improve nurture sequence, follow-up speed |
| **Cost Per Qualified Lead** | Spend ÷ qualified leads only | True lead quality | High = attracting wrong audience | Tighten ad targeting or form questions |
| **Booked Call Show-Up Rate** | % of booked calls that actually show | Nurture + confirmation effectiveness | <60% = weak nurture or wrong audience | Add confirmation emails, SMS reminders |
| **Application Completion Rate** | % who start form vs finish it | Form friction | <50% = form is too long or intimidating | Shorten form, use multi-step layout |
| **Lead-to-Sale Rate** | % of leads who become clients | Full funnel efficiency | Low = mismatch between page promise and offer | Align page messaging with sales conversation |
| **Sales Call Close Rate** | % of calls that result in sign-up | Quality of sales conversation | <20% = either wrong leads or weak close | Review sales script, objection handling |
| **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)** | Total spend ÷ new clients | Real business efficiency | Rising CPA = funnel degrading somewhere | Audit each stage individually |
| **Average Order Value (AOV)** | Average $/client | Revenue per conversion | Low AOV = under-pricing or downgrading | Test price increase, premium tiers |
| **Lifetime Value (LTV)** | Total revenue per client relationship | True ROI of acquisition | Low LTV = poor retention or ascension | Improve onboarding, results, upsell path |
| **Page Scroll Depth** | How far visitors scroll | Content engagement | Bounce at top = headline or video failing | Fix above-the-fold section first |
| **Button CTR** | % who click CTA buttons | CTA effectiveness | Low = CTA wording or placement issue | Test CTA copy, colour, placement |
| **Form Drop-Off Rate** | % who start form but don't finish | Form friction | High = too many fields or confusing UX | Reduce fields, use multi-step format |
| **Mobile vs Desktop CVR** | Conversion split by device | Mobile optimisation | Mobile CVR much lower = mobile UX issue | Fix mobile layout, button size, form |
| **Traffic Source CVR** | Conversion rate per ad platform/source | Source quality | Facebook leads convert worse = targeting issue | Adjust audience or message match per source |

## PART 9 — LANDING PAGE AUDIT CHECKLIST

### ☐ ABOVE THE FOLD
- [ ] Headline clearly states transformation + who it's for (in under 8 words)
- [ ] Subheadline adds specificity (mechanism, timeframe, or differentiator)
- [ ] Professional photo/video of you or client result is visible
- [ ] CTA button is visible without scrolling on mobile
- [ ] No navigation menu with multiple links (removes distractions)
- [ ] Page loads in under 3 seconds
- [ ] Message matches the ad that sent traffic to this page

### ☐ OFFER CLARITY
- [ ] Visitor knows within 5 seconds what you offer
- [ ] What's included is clearly listed
- [ ] Who this is for is explicitly stated
- [ ] Pricing is either shown or clearly addressed in FAQ
- [ ] The unique mechanism is stated (why your coaching is different)
- [ ] No more than one primary offer on the page

### ☐ COPYWRITING
- [ ] Copy speaks in the language your clients actually use
- [ ] Pain points are named specifically (not vaguely)
- [ ] Desired outcomes are visual and concrete
- [ ] Benefits are stated, not just features
- [ ] Copy is scannable (short paragraphs, bullet points)
- [ ] No corporate jargon or buzzwords
- [ ] Page has personality — sounds like a real human wrote it

### ☐ TRUST / PROOF
- [ ] At least 10 testimonials visible on the page
- [ ] Testimonials include specific results, not just "great coach!"
- [ ] Before/after transformation photos included
- [ ] Your credentials and background are stated
- [ ] Third-party proof (awards, media, certifications) is visible
- [ ] Client names and details (where permitted) are included

### ☐ CTA
- [ ] Primary CTA appears above the fold
- [ ] CTA is repeated at least 2–3 times down the page
- [ ] CTA button text is specific ("Apply for Coaching" not "Click Here")
- [ ] CTA is highest-contrast element on the page
- [ ] There is only ONE primary CTA goal (not multiple competing options)

### ☐ MOBILE
- [ ] Page displays correctly on iPhone and Android
- [ ] CTA buttons are large enough to tap (min 44px)
- [ ] Form fields are easy to type in on mobile keyboard
- [ ] Images are compressed for mobile load speed
- [ ] Text is readable without zooming
- [ ] Video autoplays (muted) or has a clear play button

### ☐ SPEED / TECHNICAL
- [ ] Page loads in under 3 seconds (test with PageSpeed Insights)
- [ ] Images are WebP or compressed JPG/PNG
- [ ] No broken links
- [ ] SSL certificate active (HTTPS)
- [ ] Domain is professional (not a funnel platform subdomain)
- [ ] Favicon present

### ☐ TRACKING
- [ ] Google Analytics / GA4 installed and verified
- [ ] Meta Pixel installed and firing
- [ ] Google Ads conversion tag installed (if running Google Ads)
- [ ] Form submission tracked as a conversion event
- [ ] Thank-you page or event-based conversion tracking set up
- [ ] Heatmap tool installed (Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity, etc.)
- [ ] Scroll depth tracking active

### ☐ FORM / APPLICATION
- [ ] Form is no longer than necessary (3–7 fields for most cases)
- [ ] Multi-step form used if more than 5 fields
- [ ] Pre-qualifying questions included (goal, budget readiness, experience level)
- [ ] Form confirms submission clearly
- [ ] Thank-you page sets expectations (what happens next, when they'll hear from you)
- [ ] Auto-confirmation email sent within minutes of submission

### ☐ FOLLOW-UP / NURTURE
- [ ] Auto-reply email sent immediately (confirms receipt + sets expectations)
- [ ] Follow-up SMS sent within 5 minutes of lead submission
- [ ] If no response in 24h, follow-up sequence triggers
- [ ] Call booking link included in first follow-up
- [ ] Nurture sequence continues for at least 7–14 days
- [ ] Lead is tagged in CRM based on qualifying answers
- [ ] No-show reminder sequence exists for booked calls

## PART 10 — FINAL ACTION PLAN

### Week 1: Research + Foundation

**Day 1–2:**
- Pull 20+ testimonials from existing clients (screenshots, photos, written)
- Write down the 10 most common questions/objections you hear before someone signs up
- Define your ONE core offer and its inclusions

**Day 3–4:**
- Write your hero headline using the formulas in Part 7
- Film a 60–90 second video talking directly to your ideal client (no production needed — authentic beats polished)
- Decide on HighLevel or your current platform for building

**Day 5–7:**
- Build or rebuild your landing page using the framework in Part 6
- Priority sections: Hero → Pain → Offer → Proof → FAQ → CTA
- Install Meta Pixel, GA4, and conversion tracking

---

### Week 2: Launch + Initial Data

- Drive initial traffic to the page (Meta or Google Ads — start at $30–$50/day)
- Install Hotjar or Microsoft Clarity (free heatmap tool) to watch scroll depth and click behaviour
- Set up your immediate follow-up sequence: auto-reply email + SMS within 5 minutes
- Track: CPL, application completion rate, booked call rate

---

### Week 3–4: First Optimisation Round

**What to look at:**
- Scroll depth: If people are leaving at the top, fix the headline and video
- Form drop-off: If people start the form but don't finish, reduce fields or use multi-step
- Low booked call rate: If applications aren't converting to calls, improve thank-you page and follow-up speed
- Low show-up rate: Add SMS reminders 24h and 1h before the call

**What to test first (priority order):**
1. Headline (biggest lever)
2. Hero image/video
3. CTA button text
4. Form length
5. Testimonial placement

---

### 30-Day Optimisation Plan

| Days | Focus |
|---|---|
| 1–7 | Build and launch page, install tracking |
| 8–14 | Collect data — minimum 200+ visitors before judging |
| 15–20 | Review heatmaps, form analytics, and conversion rate |
| 21–25 | Test one headline variant (keep everything else the same) |
| 26–30 | Review results, implement winner, plan next test |

**Key principle (confirmed from Brandon Willington):** You don't need to rebuild your page. 1–2 specific changes, based on data, will often double your conversion rate. Start small, test one thing at a time, let the data guide you.

---

### Ongoing Priority Metrics to Review Weekly
1. Landing page CVR (are more visitors becoming leads?)
2. CPL (is each lead costing more or less?)
3. Show-up rate (are the right people booking calls?)
4. Close rate (are calls converting to clients?)

Fix whichever is weakest first. That's the Brandon Willington / Where U diagnostic framework in practice.
