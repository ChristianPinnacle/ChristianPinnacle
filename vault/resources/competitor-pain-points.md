# Competitor Pain Points & User Sentiment — July 2026

> Source: live web research (July 2026) across app store reviews, Trustpilot, G2, Capterra, GetApp, Reddit-sourced reporting, and 2026 review roundups. Supersedes June 2025 competitive intelligence for sentiment data. Use alongside `market-intelligence.md`.

---

## 1. MEAL TRACKING / NUTRITION APPS

### MyFitnessPal (market leader, actively bleeding users)
**Love:**
- Largest food database (14M+ items), best integration breadth (50+ apps/devices)
- Brand recognition — "log it in MFP" is default vocabulary
- Recipe URL import (now Premium-only)

**Hate (2026-specific, high-intensity):**
- **April 2026 "Today" tab redesign** triggered a wave of cancellations/refund requests. Complaints: week defaults to Sunday, swiping between days removed, food logs buried behind "View All", nutrition info harder to access. Company confirmed redesign is permanent. Users found a workaround (changing account region) — that's how much they hate it.
- **May 2026 paywall expansion**: scan-a-meal, recipe URL import, and macro-by-meal goals moved behind Premium ($79.99/yr / $19.99/mo). Follows 2022 barcode-scanner paywall. Perceived as bait-and-switch after years of loyalty.
- Free tier now caps at 5 food entries/day — effectively a demo.
- Database accuracy: user-submitted entries; study found saturated fat underestimated 13–40%, cholesterol 26–60%. r/loseit and r/CICO routinely warn to verify against USDA.
- Logging speed: 60–120s per item, 3–5 min per meal — vs <10s for AI apps.
- Ad-heavy free tier: full-screen interstitials between logging, banners everywhere.
- Poor Apple Health sync (missing food names, wrong timestamps).
- Bought Cal AI (Mar 2026) — folding photo-AI into Premium as the upsell hook. Consolidating competitors instead of fixing the product.

**Want added:** fast AI logging without paying, verified data, the old UI back.

### MacroFactor
**Love:**
- Adaptive TDEE algorithm (the moat) — recalculates expenditure weekly from intake + weight trend; detects metabolic adaptation. No competitor matches it.
- Verified NCC-derived database — no user-submitted garbage.
- No guilt-based UX (no red warning zones), no ads, no gamification.
- Coached/collaborative/manual macro modes. Evidence-based credibility (Stronger By Science).
- Fast logging workflow, remembers meal-slot habits, AI describe-your-meal feature.

**Hate:**
- No free tier (~US$72/yr) — biggest adoption barrier.
- Algorithm punishes inconsistent logging — skip a few days and expenditure estimates go unreliable. Hostile to beginners.
- Limited micronutrient visibility (~15–32 nutrients vs Cronometer's 84).
- No wearable standalone logging (no Apple Watch/Wear OS app).
- No recipe URL import — manual ingredient-by-ingredient recipe building.
- Onboarding assumes you know TDEE/macros — overwhelming for beginners.
- Database coverage geographically uneven outside North America (relevant: AU brands).
- Photo AI logging added Apr 2025 but reviewers say it lags dedicated AI-first apps; some 2026 AI features called "controversial" by long-term reviewers.

### Cronometer
**Love:**
- 82–84 nutrient tracking — the only consumer app that takes micros seriously. Dietitian/clinical gold standard; verified NCCDB/USDA data flagged separately from user entries.
- Genuinely usable free tier (full nutrient tracking, barcode, recipe builder). Gold is cheap ($49.99/yr).
- CSV export of full history (rare). Blood work integration on paid tier (note: they're adjacent to your bloodwork thesis).

**Hate:**
- Manual-only logging in practice; photo logging (Gold) rated "borderline unusable" vs AI-first apps. 45–90s per item; 3-item meal = 2–5 min.
- Dated, dense interface — "feels like homework"; users either love it for years or quit within a month. Poor onboarding.
- Weak for restaurant meals and composite dishes; US-oriented database — UK users report consistently incorrect macros for local brands (expect same for AU).
- Slow web app; support tickets ignored for paid users (resolved only via Reddit escalation).

### Category baseline (2026)
- **AI photo/voice/text logging is now table stakes.** Apps without it are described as "dated" in every 2026 review. A wave of cheap AI-first trackers (Cal AI, GAYA, Hoot, Nutrola, PlateLens, Vora, etc.) is harvesting MFP churn at $2.50–$10/mo.
- Verified database > big database. Every serious 2026 review dings crowd-sourced data.
- Users choose replacement app by naming their #1 friction: slow logging → AI app; paywall → FatSecret/Lose It; accuracy → Cronometer/MacroFactor; adaptive coaching → MacroFactor.

## 2. COACH PLATFORMS

### Kahunas (direct competitor — AU/UK coach market)
**Love (coach-side):**
- Branded app in app stores = the headline reason coaches choose it (premium feel for high-ticket coaching).
- Check-in system, custom daily habit forms, progress photo overlays, voice notes, everything-in-one-pipeline.
- No commission on payments. Outstanding, fast support team (dominates their Trustpilot 5★).

**Hate (client-side — this is the wedge):**
- Nutrition described as "abysmal" in Google Play reviews: poor food search (items disappear as you type), barcode scanner misses ~half of items, slow to delete logged items, can't edit foods saved in "My Foods".
- App crashes/kicks users out mid-workout repeatedly; bug reports unresolved for months.
- Coaches: "nutrition section is slightly behind competitors like MFP. Cannot build and save meals."
- Platform stagnation: 2025–26 feature rollout slowed (team cites "platform stability" work). One scathing Trustpilot review: no improvement in 2+ years, clients leaving because of the app, founder unresponsive when things go wrong.
- **Lock-in resentment:** coaches say they stay only because extracting their data/programs is too hard. Little flexibility to adapt platform to your coaching style.

### Everfit
**Love:** best-in-class workout builder (drag-drop, %1RM auto-progression, supersets/AMRAP), Master Planner, clean UI, fast onboarding, automation at scale (Autoflow), strong wearable coverage (incl. Oura + Google Fit), responsive support, free 5-client tier, Everfit Academy.

**Hate:**
- **Inadequate nutrition** — the #1 and #2 negative themes on G2 (17 + 12 mentions): missing macro tracking depth, fibre and micronutrients not visible in-app, coaches forced back to MFP for detail. Meal planning is a paid add-on and still weak.
- Add-on pricing = "death by a thousand cuts": solo coach with 50 clients wanting workouts + meal plans + automation ≈ US$134+/mo.
- Weak lifetime/long-term client data trends. Payments unsupported in some countries. White-label pricey. Not HIPAA compliant.

### ABC Trainerize
**Love:** deep workout builder (AMRAP/EMOM/%1RM), huge integration ecosystem, auto-scheduled messages, custom branded app ($169 one-off), habit coaching, used by big chains.

**Hate:**
- Slow and clunky; reported outages locking coaches AND clients out for hours to a full day.
- Bugs/crashes/sync issues on mobile, chronic MFP-sync problems, glitchy custom branding.
- Add-on stacking: real solo-coach cost is $100–200/mo, not the $23/mo headline (Pro $120 + Advanced Nutrition $45 + Stripe $10 ≈ $175/mo for 50 clients).
- AI Workout Builder struggles with complex progressions.
- Post-ABC-acquisition decline narrative: "dated", billing errors, unresponsive support, decade-long customers leaving. Exercise library search "an absolute joke" for basic movements.

### TrueCoach
**Love:** fastest free-form programming builder, copy/paste whole weeks, 3,000+ video library, client video feedback (form checks), clean client app, strong support.

**Hate:**
- Nutrition = MFP integration only, and it shows **macros only — no micros, no food log detail**. Their "AI Meal Plan Generator" is literally a Notion template, not a product feature.
- New **5% transaction fee on all client payments (Jan 2026)** — at $2k/mo billing that's $100/mo on top of subscription. Widely resented.
- Pricing cliff: 20 clients $58/mo → 50 clients $137/mo, nothing in between.
- Coach mobile app can't program/edit (web-only workflow); Android app particularly broken (search doesn't work, redirects to website).
- Stock exercise videos show novice form — coaches re-record everything.
- Stagnation: "only thing they updated in 5 years is the price." Coaches churn when they need habit automation, structured check-ins, and nutrition coaching for online-first businesses.
- Payments limited to US/UK/CA/AU.

### My PT Hub
**Love:** unlimited clients at flat price (big draw vs per-client pricing), all-in-one coverage, nutrition section rated among the best of the coach platforms, responsive support, white label.

**Hate:**
- Built-in nutrition database (600k items) is "a little buggy" per coaches; nutritionists report **no visible micronutrient data for trainers** — forced to another platform.
- Workout building slow/clunky: broken search, duplicate exercises, lost progress, inflexible editing ("too many bugs, too inflexible" — cited as reason for switching).
- Glitchy chat; voice messages fail to send or record as 0s.
- Payments: Stripe-only, slow transfers, high fees, clunky invoicing/cancellation.
- 12-month lock-in (no month-to-month), dismissive response to critical feedback.

### PT Distinction
**Love:** easiest workout builder of the category per switchers, group automation, no fee for app customisation, strong support/community, powerful analytics, sells automated coaching packages.

**Hate:** no native meal-plan creation in-app (relies on MFP integration — inherits all MFP problems), coach app is client-only (coaches stuck on web), steep learning curve without training videos, limited smart-device integrations (no Garmin etc.).

### Consumer workout loggers (what individual lifters use: Hevy, Strong)
Relevant because these set client-side UX expectations for T3/T4.
- **Hevy loved:** fastest logging, native supersets/drop sets/rest-pause, RPE/RIR fields, free tier depth, social feed, offline logging with sync.
- **Strong loved:** Apple Watch phone-free logging, CSV export, analytics depth, plate calculator.
- **Both hated for:** being loggers not coaches — no periodization, no adaptive planning, no recovery data (HRV/sleep), **no coach integration**, and neither touches food. Lifters juggle a tracker + a calorie app and resent it. Timer bugs are Hevy's most common complaint.
- Takeaway: your client training module competes with Hevy-grade logging UX. Match: fast set entry, rest timer that works, supersets/drop sets, RPE, offline queue (already in T4 scope), history/PRs. Beat: coach visibility + nutrition in the same app.

## 3. BLOOD ANALYSIS PLATFORMS

### InsideTracker
**Love:** science-backed interpretation with study citations, upload-your-own-labs ($149/yr membership analyses external bloodwork), wearable integrations (Oura/Whoop/Garmin/Apple), goal-based action plans, new Terra AI guide answering questions about your results.
**Hate:** **price is the #1 complaint** (~US$1,509/yr at quarterly cadence), technical issues uploading blood test data, inconsistent support, InnerAge accuracy doubts, email spam, credits expiring. US-only draws (Quest).

### Function Health
**Love:** breadth (160+ tests/yr) at $499, best-in-class results dashboard (colour-coded, per-biomarker explanations), catches issues doctors missed, HSA-eligible, 200k+ subscribers — validates consumer demand for biomarker tracking.
**Hate:**
- **"Hands you a complex medical document and leaves you to figure it out"** — no consultation included, DIY interpretation, clinician notes described as broad/generic and delayed (up to 4 weeks; some report months).
- Aggressive supplement cross-promotion undermines trust in recommendations.
- Lab-visit friction (two fasted Quest visits, 85mL+ blood), lost results horror stories, refund refusals, support unresponsive. Trustpilot much weaker than marketing suggests.
- Standard reference ranges, not optimal/performance ranges — the exact gap performance coaches fill.

### Marek Health
**Love:** the humans — assigned health coach + provider consult to interpret labs and build a protocol; monthly check-ins; genuine 5★ Trustpilot (900+ reviews). Optimal-range, performance-oriented interpretation. TRT/peptides/GLP-1 delivery.
**Hate/limits:** US-only telehealth, requires annual in-person physical, LabCorp visits, panels $250–$2,000. It's a clinic, not software — doesn't scale coach workflows.

### SiPhox Health
**Love:** at-home finger-prick convenience, upload external results, per-marker explanations, affordable, human walkthrough of results.
**Hate:** kit shipping issues, result delays breaking trend-tracking, difficulty self-collecting samples, mixed support.

### Category takeaways
- Consumer demand for biomarker tracking is proven and large (Function: 200k subs, ~$100M ARR).
- The universal gap: **interpretation + action**. Data-only products get slammed for DIY overwhelm; human-guided ones (Marek) win loyalty but don't scale and don't exist for the coach channel.
- Nobody offers coach-facing bloodwork workflow, and nobody serves Australia well. Standard-vs-optimal ranges is the positioning language coaches already use.
- Upload-your-own-labs (InsideTracker/SiPhox pattern) is the low-friction entry — validates the planned PDF/image auto-parse feature as the on-ramp, no lab partnership required.

## 4. CROSS-CUTTING PATTERNS

1. **Every coach platform's #1 weakness is nutrition depth.** Kahunas (abysmal client logging), Everfit (no micros, add-on meal plans), TrueCoach (macros-only via MFP), Trainerize ($45/mo nutrition add-on, broken MFP sync), My PT Hub (buggy DB, no micros for trainers), PT Distinction (no native meal plans). Coaches openly run a second nutrition app. VitalEdge's nutrition-first architecture attacks the seam every incumbent has.
2. **Every nutrition app's weakness is zero coach visibility** (Cronometer Pro is the lone partial exception and it's clinical, clunky, and has no training side).
3. **AI logging is table stakes for clients in 2026.** Photo + voice + natural-language entry. This is the biggest client-facing gap to close in VitalEdge.
4. **Verified/localised database beats big database.** AU brand coverage is a differentiator vs US-centric Cronometer/MacroFactor and dirty MFP data.
5. **Pricing resentment patterns to exploit:** add-on stacking (Everfit, Trainerize), transaction fees (TrueCoach 5%), per-client cliffs (TrueCoach 20→50), retroactive paywalls (MFP). Flat, honest, everything-included weekly pricing is a selling point — say so explicitly on the coach landing page.
6. **Lock-in is hated; portability wins switchers.** Kahunas coaches stay only because they can't extract data. The client migration/import wizard (CSV from MFP/Trainerize/TrueCoach) directly weaponises this — market it as "leave in an afternoon."
7. **Stability > features.** Trainerize outages, Kahunas crashes, My PT Hub bugs are churn drivers. "It just works" is a legitimate claim if the platform stays clean (keep the zero-TS-errors discipline as a marketing fact).
8. **Never repeat MFP's redesign mistake:** don't bury the diary, don't remove day-swiping, don't change defaults without opt-out. Loud lesson from 2026.
9. **Support is a moat for small players.** Kahunas and My PT Hub retain coaches on support quality alone despite product flaws. Founder-led support is a stage-appropriate advantage.

## 5. FEATURE PRIORITY IMPLICATIONS FOR VITALEDGE

**Client-side (retention/word-of-mouth):**
- AI photo/voice/text food logging (table stakes; MFP paywalled it — offer it included)
- Fast, reliable food search + AU-localised verified entries; editable saved foods; build-and-save meals (all Kahunas failures)
- Hevy-grade workout logging: rest timer, supersets/drop sets, RPE, offline queue (T4), PR history
- Never crash mid-workout; offline-first logging

**Coach-side (acquisition/differentiation):**
- Full macro + micro visibility into client nutrition (no coach platform has it)
- Bloodwork upload → auto-parse → optimal-range interpretation → coach-reviewed protocol (Function's data + Marek's human layer, delivered through THEIR coach — nobody does this)
- Import wizard as switching weapon; export freedom as trust signal
- Flat everything-included pricing messaging vs add-on/fee resentment
- Long-term client trend views (Everfit gap)

**Individual users:**
- Adaptive target adjustment (MacroFactor's moat — auto macro adjustment on diet phase change is already scoped; extend toward weight-trend-driven adjustments over time)
- Beginner-friendly onboarding (MacroFactor/Cronometer both fail beginners)
